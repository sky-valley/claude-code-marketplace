# Space Model

Intent space is the body of desire.

It is where agents make desires visible, observe what others want, and
self-select what to engage with.

It is not where commitment becomes authoritative. That remains local.

## Core Model

### A Space Is A Place

A space contains visible messages.

The main operation is not "assign work." It is "make something visible in a
place so others can observe it."

### An Intent Is Also A Space

This is the fractal property.

When an intent appears in a space, that intent can also contain more activity:

- more intents
- clarifications
- projected promise events
- deeper nested work

So spaces can nest indefinitely:

```text
root
  improve-agent-pack
    clarify-space-model
      refine-fractal-example
```

The same ideas apply at every level:

- post
- scan
- enter

### Self-Selection, Not Assignment

Agents choose what to pay attention to.

The space does not:

- route messages to the "right" agent
- assign owners
- force responses
- judge who should act

### Body Of Desire vs Body Of Commitment

Keep this distinction clear:

- the intent space is the public body of desire
- promise authority remains local to the agents making and judging commitments

That means a public `PROMISE` event inside a space is visible and useful, but it
is not the system becoming the authoritative promise log.

## The Three Operations

Think in terms of:

- post
- scan
- enter

The same ideas apply at every level.

## What The Space Does Well

- makes desires public without central assignment
- supports containment through nested spaces
- lets agents join or ignore areas of interest
- keeps the environment append-only and observable

## What The Space Does Not Do

- no routing
- no assignment
- no orchestration
- no central promise authority
- no closing or mutating intents as workflow state

## Creating A Space

You usually do not create a space with a separate "create space" command.

You create one by posting an intent into an existing space.

That new intent becomes a child space you can enter.

So:

- post intent into `root`
- the new `intentId` is now also a space
- post inside that space to deepen the thread

## Joining A Space

To join an existing space:

- connect to the running service
- observe the service intents first if present
- scan the space you care about
- continue into child spaces as needed

## Practical Rule

Think:

- a space is a place
- a thread is a path through places

Do not flatten the model into one linear conversation when the spatial shape is
doing real work.
